Case Study: UPI QR Code Scam

What the Crime is: > This is a form of social engineering fraud where scammers trick victims into authorizing a "debit" transaction under the guise of receiving a "credit" payment via UPI (Unified Payments Interface).

Step-by-Step Breakdown:

1. The Hook: The scammer contacts a victim (often someone selling an item on platforms like OLX) pretending to be a buyer.

2. The Deception: They claim they want to pay an advance and send a QR code via WhatsApp.

3. The Trap: The scammer tells the victim, "Scan this code to receive money in your bank account."

4. The Theft: When the victim scans the code and enters their UPI PIN, they are actually authorizing a payment to the scammer rather than receiving one. Money is instantly debited from the victim's account.

Target Audience: > College students, small-scale online sellers, and individuals not well-versed in the technical workings of UPI.

Consequences: > Victims face immediate financial loss. Since UPI transactions are instantaneous and irreversible, recovering the funds is extremely difficult. Beyond the money, victims often feel a loss of confidence in digital banking.
